A monk order based around spirit animals. Spirit animal primarily protects the monk.

Weapon and skilll
Unarmed, Thrown Blade, Club
Athletics, Lore, Survival

Aura: Vicious Aura
Anytime a creature within your aura is successfully attacked, you may use your reaction to inflict a biting attack with a bonus to determining if it is a critical hit equal to heroism

Initiate: When an attack against you is a critical hit, you instead ignore it. You must fulfil a long or full rest before doing so again.
When you perform an unarmed attack, you may treat it as a biting attack.

Apprentice: You may spend a whole round to transform into your spirit form. Treat as a bear's stats except your unarmed attacks are considered magical and the size may be small, medium, or large as appropriate to the creature. Transforming back requires a whole round. If you are wounded in that form while it has no hitpoints, you will immediately transform back and receive the wound in your natural form and receive a point of fatigue. Damage dealt to your spirit form is retained when you transform back, but is recovered normally by resting (even in your natural form). Transforming more than twice between rests incurs a level of fatigue each time.
Your aura(s) are still active in tour spirit form.

Journeyman: You may treat the follow up attacks of your standard or spirit form as biting attacks.
The first time you perform an incapacitation save, if your other form still has all of its hitpoints, you add your heroism to tour save. You must complete a long rest before doing so again.
Your spirit form's hitpoints increase by 1. 
You gain a level of training in unarmed attacks.
You retain your weapon training in your spirit form.
Choose a valder spell. (nce this decision is made it cannot be changed. While in your spiritform you may cast it as a wyrding with a bonus equal to your level, but must complete a fullrest before doing so again.
When you recoil, you may shift the wound result a number of steps equal to your spirit form's remaining hitpoints.
