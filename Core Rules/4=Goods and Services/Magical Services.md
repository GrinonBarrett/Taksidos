### Magical Services
Magical services are grouped by the basic nature of its function, rather than a particular associated talent.

##### Cartomancy
Various forms of divination using cards. They can provide general advice or warning against a course of action. A session can vary in price from practitioner to practitioner, not to mention that more prestigious clients seem to pay more for a reading. Interestingly, it seems that this is a particular service that becomes more expensive as you reach denser cities with more offering their services. A local woman performing readings as a side business might charge as little as $60 while some "specialists" might charge up to ten times as much!

##### Cryptogast
A service of traditional nekromanteirs (not to be confused with necromancers). They will send a message of a single sentance to a creature you are familiar with and describe. This has a percentage chance of failing equal to 50 + triple the word count. Additionally, the recipient can only receive the message when in a place of reverence to the dead or when they awake from either a particularly pleasant dream or a nightmare. This can take up to 1d4 days. If the nessage is not received in time, it s lost. No one knows exactly what is required for this except the practitioner and such messages will cost 1d12+18 x 750. The cost and unreliability make this a deperate measure for communication.

##### Curse Tablets
Made of a few ounces of led, the inscriber drafts some ill words by impression into the tablet. The tablet is to be burried either under the intended subject's home or an important place contextually for the curse and subject. Successful application of the tablet can potentially cause a -1 to all checks and saves as appropriate to the curse and subject and an additional -1 for the purposes of determining if a failure is a critical failure. A tablet can be obtained relatively cheap. A good deal would be $30-40 with some more elaborate tablets being up to $450. Strangely, they don't seem to affect fey.

##### Enchanting
Some magical armorers can offer temporary enchantments for non-magical weapons and armor. This works for 1d4+3 days. If such a temporary enchantment is to affect a weapon, it will cost $2,000. If a shield, $675. If armor, $1,400. If a piece of ammunition, it is $500 though the use of that ammunition (whether a successful attack or not) will also rid it of the magic.

##### Esferaquiency
A form of divination with uses a number of glass marbles. Traditionally, 12 clear marbles, 12 black marbles, 12 white marbles, 12 clear marbles with metalic dust trapped inside, and a special tiger's eye marble which is capable of changing color with temperature. The subject is to hold the special marble on their person while speaking to the diviner. This conversation is to include the client's name, a deity they intend to ask, the spirit with which they seek the knowledge, and the subject of their inquiry. The remaining marbles are placed in a jar with a closed lid and shaken vigorously. A silver basin of water is still between the diviner and client. The client blindly casts their marble into basin- at which point the divener immediately pours the remaining marbles. The diviner interprets the relationship of the marbles to either other and the coloration of the unique marble to divine a vague potential outcome of a course of action or the favor of the invoked deity. Such readings run between $1,000 - $2,200, they the client retains their particular marble as it is considered to be a reminder of the the reading lest the client act in a worse way.

##### Gold Blessing Tablets
Made of gold, they contain words of positivity and some invocation. If placed over the door of one's abode or doorway of their room, it is said to offer protection for the home and its occupants. It can provide a +1 to saves against magical effects this way. Such a tablet sets you back $1d4+4 × 100.

##### Goust Tag
A piece of parchment which contains written wards against unseen entities. The ink is itself blessed by some priest or made of blessed components. It discourages mindless ephemera from moving neary to them, often staying up to 5 yards away. Non-mindless ephemera have been known to ignore them.

##### Luck Charm
A simple totem or trinket which is intended to ward bad luck from afflicting the wearer. If a save/check by the user would be a critical failure, the charm provides a +1 bonus for the purpose of determining if it is a failure or critical failure. The wearer must complete a long or full rest before it can do so again. Some charms are made for luck involving certain subjects, such charms only are tighered by saves/checks involving that subject. A charm like this can set you back $1d10+1d12+1d20+2. Unlike typical amulets, there is technically no limit to how many of these charms can be active at once- but you cannot benefit from more charms than your heroism (minimum of 1) on a single save.

##### Relief Magic
Folk magic, usually including use of salve and incantation which can ease pain. Prices are typicall $1d20+15 but can be much greater. There isn't much in game mechanical benefit to this, but the narrator may have ways of representing the benefits of not suffering so much. It is not uncommon for healers and miwives to have familiarity with this.

#### Auspicy
The following services are each a form of divination which are considered imperfect. Many are affected by the medium through which the divination occurs. Because these magics are easier to perform, they are far less expensive. One that performs this service may be called and an Auspice, Auspex, Ergefsuh, or rarely a Diviner.

##### Augery
The practitioner seeks to gain guidance by interpreting the movements of birds. General yes/no sorts of answers can be scried, as well as the intensity of an answer. A general direction may also be obtained from a sharp movement of a leading bird. Used for general omens typically, this service can set one back at least $70, but some charge as much as $175 depending on the nature of the request and the status of the auspex.

##### Pyropropheticism
The practitioner seeks to divine a result from flame by interpreting the color, direction, and scent of the smoke. Typically, a scenario is proposed regarding the future or an event. What is revealed is whether the result will be good/bad or as intended and to what degree it is so.  
An average session can cause the player to part with $50-$200, depending on the practitioner and the nature of their request.

One can expect general guidance as to the direction one should go in when it comes to decision making. Occasionally, extremely intense feedback can be obtained. In these cases, the auspex may actually get a vision and more than they bargained for.

Many practictioners of this form of divination are Ashen Sisters, a sorority of diviners who worship a fire goddess.

##### Haruspicy
The practitioner slaughters an animal and reads signs through the organs and remains. A sick animal will greatly corrupt the result. Average cost is two and a half times that of the animal slaughtered.
