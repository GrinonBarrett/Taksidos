### Petroleaphage
A silky amber-black ooze which glistens with rainbow refraction when light catches the right angle. They feed on lantern oil and similar compounds. Their collective noun is a spill.

**Creature Type**: Petroleaphages are Infigura.

**Size**: Petroleaphages range from 9" to 9' in height, being thrice as long. They can weigh up to 35 pounds. They can be diminutive, small, medium, or large creatures.

**Languages**: Petroleaphages can't speak.

-----

**Hitpoints**: A petroleaphage has a number of hitpoints equal to its weight in pounds divided by 5, plus 3 additional hitpoints.

**Attributes**:  
[Body] 8 (+2)  
[Fort] 7 (+2)  
[Rflx] 7 (+2)  
[Will] 7 (+2)  
[Inte] 2 (+0)  
[Prcp] 5 (+1)  
[Empy] 5 (+1)  

**Run Speed**: It may run up to 2.5 yards per move.

**Climb Speed**: It may climb up to 1.5 yards per move, presuming it is reasonable terrain to do so.

**Swim Speed**: It may swim .5 yard per move, so long as the water isn't too rough.

**Unarmed Attacks**;

 • Whack: The petroleaphage may manipulate its body to inflict a melee bare attack which may only be performed against a grappled foe.

#### Special Features 

**Amorphous**: Pteroleaphages don’t retain a single cohesive shape but are instead in the form of a glob of gooey material. This confers some strange properties to it.  
 • Immunity to falling and knockback damage.  
 • Obstacles that would reduce the speed of it have only half of their normal effect unless they specifically affect amorphous creatures as well.  
 • All forms of non-magical attack against it have disadvantage.  
 • Anytime a spell would be cast against it that would affect its form, shape, composition, or mental state- it gains a d12 bonus to its saves against that effect.  
 • It cannot be grappled.  
 • While it has a creature grappled, it can treat that creature as being unarmored for the purpose of attacking it.  
 • It is capable of traveling through any opening small enough to stick one’s fist.  
 • Cannot use armor, shields, or any item.   
 • Receives wounds, but no effect from them.

 **Infigural Nature**: Being infigura, petroleaphages have several advantages.  
 • Immunity to all poisons and diseases, including toxic damage, except those that specifically affect infigura. Magic attacks are not affected by this.  
 • Immunity to the effects of bloodloss.  
 • Immunity to fatigue from outside sources.  
 • Immunity to sleep.  
 • Immunity to effects which cause asphyxiation.  
 • Immunity to effects which would inflict the prone condition, unless it specifically targets infigura.  
 • Does not require sleep to rest, but must still manage to relax and unwind as other creatures would.  
 • Immunity to incapacitation, but is destroyed when wounded with no remaining hitpoints.

**Minimal Sensing**: It is, in the most technical sense, blind and deaf. Instead it relies on sensing vibrations, smelling, and its ability to taste everything it comes in contact with. Anytime it would attempt a check or save based on perceiving something, it has disadvantage (likewise, it should get advantage against illusions based on such stimuli). If it would rely on multiple missing senses, it suffers an additional -2 penalty for each total sense. If it is touching or tasting something, it does not have any issues identifying it.

**Mindless**: Petroleaphages do not have capacity for independent thought. As such, they cannot participate in articulate thought or coordination. They are also unaffected by mind-influencing effects and fear-based effects.

**Resistances**: Bare and cold attacks of a non-magical nature against the petroleaphage have disadvantage.

**Vulnerablity**: If half of an ounce of sudokahest makes contact with a petroleaphage, it inflicts an acid attack with advantage against the creature. If half of an ounce of alkahest makes contact with a petroleophage, it will permanently reduce its maximum hitpoints by 1d4. It then receives a number of acid attacks equal to 4 minus the die roll result. These are made with advantage and a bonus equal to the die roll result.

**Flammability of a Petroleaphage**: Fire attacks against the petroleaphage have disadvantage unless it is currently in possession of as much oil as half of its own weight. If it has two-thirds as much oil as its own weight, fire attacks have advantage against it.

**Momentous Form**: Every size category of the creature above diminutive increases its speeds by 1 yard each.

**Floater**: A petroleaphage always floats on top of water.
