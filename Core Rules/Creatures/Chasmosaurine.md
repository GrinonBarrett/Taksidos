### Chasmosaurine
Representing a number of similar creatures, chasmosaurines are large herbivore with 2 or 3 horns and large neck frills. There is a great variety to their individual appearance and their habitat stretches along much of the western lands. When single out for an attack, they will often charge a foe and threaten them with their horns (or the sheer weight of trampling if a smaller foe). This may also force an opening by which it can escape. In a group they will often from lines shoulder to should with some of the younger ones sprinting around a foe as a cooperative means to control the engagement. Their collective noun is a Herd.

**Creature Type**: Chasmosaurines are Beasts.

**Size**: Adult chasmosaurines can be between 20'-30'" in length and frequently stand between 3'3"-4' at the shoulder. They weigh between 1,540-3,525 pounds. They are large creatures.

**Hitpoints**: A chasmosaurines has 6 hitpoints.

**Attributes**:  
[Body] 19 (+6)  
[Fort] 12 (+4)  
[Rflx] 12 (+4)  
[Will] 15 (+5)  
[Inte] 8 (+2)  
[Prcp] 11 (+3)  
[Empy] 10 (+3)  

**Languages**: Chasmosaurines don't speak.

**Run Speed**: It may run up to 6 yards per move.

**Swim speed**: It may swim up to 1 yard per move, presuming the water isn’t too rough.

**Unarmed Attacks**;

 • Smash: The chasmosaurine may use any part of its body to strike a foe within .5 yard. This is a melee bare attack.

 • Gore: The kentrosaurus may us its tail against any creature behind or among one of two its sides within 2 yards. This is treated as either a melee bare attack or melee piercing attack.

**Thagomizer Precision**: Presuming it is not prone, if a kentrosaurus strikes a foe with its Thagomizer attack, it may use its reaction to inflict an additional piercing attack against the foe.

**Spiked Defense**: If a melee attack is made against the kentrosaurus within 1 yard of the kentrosaurus, it may use its reaction to inflict a piercing attack against that creature. If foe's attack was a critical failure, it gains advantage on its attack.  
Additionally, a creature of equal or greater size which attempts to brawl or trample the kentrosaurus is subject to a piercing attack.
