### Shorehound
This creature resmbles a canine with leathery skin in place of fur and a tortoise shell. Where one would expect its rear paws are instead flippers and its front paws resemble webbed claws. Their collective noun is a pack.

**Creature Type**: Shorehounds are Bigenerae. They are affected by magic as though they were Reptiles.

**Size**: Shorehounds stand between 1'7"-2' at the shoulder and are between 3' and 4' length. The weight is usually between 55 and 85 pounds depending on breed. They care small creatures.

**Hitpoints**: A shorehound has no hitpoints.

**Attributes**:  
[Body] 9  (+3)  
[Fort] 10 (+3)  
[Rflx] 12 (+4)  
[Will] 9  (+3)  
[Inte] 9  (+3)  
[Prcp] 10 (+3)  
[Empy] 10 (+3)  

**Languages**: Shorhounds don't speak, but can convey very basic information by barking.

**Run Speed**: It may run up to 35 yards per move.

**Swim speed**: It may swim up to 9 yards per move, presuming the water isn’t too rough.

**Unarmed Attacks**;

 • Tackle: The shorehound may use its body to strike a foe within 1 foot. This is a melee bare attack.

 • Bite: The shorhound may perform a single bite against a foe within 1 foot. This is a melee bite attack.

 • Claw: The shorehound may use one of its front claws to trick a foe within 1 foot, but has disadvantage. This is a melee cutting attack.

**Keen Oralfactory Senses**: The shorehound can breathe in water just like it were air.

**Constant Mover**: The first time it would sprint during a round, the shorehound does not incur fatigue.

**Thunderous Voice**: As an action, the shorhound can loose a loud roarous bark thich can be heard quite a ways away. Any creature within 7 yards or a 15 yard cone is subject to a sonic attack. Such an attack has disadvantage against other shorehounds. It must rest before doing so again.

**Tuck in Shell**: A shorehound may retreat into (or come out of) its shell as an action. While in its shell, the shorehound is considered prone. Additionally, its defense is increased by 4. Furthermore, the only wounds which may be dealt to it are biased wounds. If it has no such wounds options remaining, it is no longer limited to only receiving biased wounds.

#### Gaining a Shorehound Servile

An invoker which has earned the trust of a shorehound can simply entreat it into their serivce as a lesser servile. When entreated into service this way, it is treated as having a single hitpoint.

**Dominion Benefits**: The shorehound gains the benefit as appropriate to your domain.  
* **Chosen Spirit**. Once per encounter, when the shorehound tucks into its shell, *it may instantly appear at a point within a number of yards up to the invoker's level*.
* **The Earth**. The shorehound gains a +2 bonmus to defense against non-magical attacks.
* **The Flame**. The shorehound is capable of casting the spell Dragon's Breath as though it could perform wyrdings.
* **The Grave**. The shorehound is capable of casting the spell Rotword as though it could perform wyrdings.
* **The Light**: As an action, it may choose to emit dim light out a number of feet equal to the invoker's conjury.
* **Nobility**. It gains a +2 bonus to defense against magical attacks.
* **The Sea**. Cold and crushing attacks have disadvantage against the shorehound.
* **The Sky**. Electric and cutting attacks have disadvantage against the shorehound.
* **Valdercaller**. The shorehound is capable of casting the spel Waedervalder as though it could perform wyrdings.
