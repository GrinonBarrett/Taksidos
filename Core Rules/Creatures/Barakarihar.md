### Barakarihar
When a body of water is used to dispose of the victims of violent crimes, their negative energy will pool and rot- birthing a particularly malignant fiend. These beings seek to avenge those who have been deprived of a proper end by their dismissal within the water. Once they have avenged those fallen, they will then be so overcome by the inability to find peace that they will become outwardly destructive to any sign of life. The plural is Barakarihain. Their collective noun is a tide.

**Creature Type**: Barakarihain are Fiend creatures.

**Size**: When not in an aqueous form, a barakarihar can be between 5-7 feet tall and weigh 200 pounds.

**Languages**: Barakarihain speak Hydroglossa and the language of the last words of each creature disposed of in its waters.

**Hitpoints**: A barakarihar has 7 hitpoints.

**Attributes**:  
[Body] 16 (+5)  
[Fort] 15 (+5)  
[Rflx] 7 (+2)  
[Will] 17 (+5)  
[Inte] 4 (+1)  
[Prcp] 9 (+3)  
[Empy] 9 (+3)  

**Run Speed**: It may run up to 6 yards per move.

**Swim speed**: It may swim up to 30 yards per move.

**Unarmed Attacks**;

 • Strike: The barakarihar may use any part of its body to strike a foe within 1 yard, inflicting a club attack and a toxic attack.

#### Special Features

**Amorphous Fiend**: Barakarihain are toxic embodiments of water. As such they;  
 • Do not need to eat, sleep, or breathe. They do still need to relax to rest.  
 • Are immune to diseases, poisons and venom.  
 • Immune to the effects of bloodloss.  
 • Are not affected by spells which would affect flesh.  
 • Receives wounds, but no effect from them.  
 • Immunity to falling and knockback damage.  
 • Obstacles that would reduce the speed of it have only half of their normal effect unless they specifically affect amorphous creatures as well.  
 • Anytime a spell would be cast against it that would affect its form, shape, composition, or mental state- it gains a d12 bonus to its saves against that effect.  
 • It cannot be grappled.  
 • While it has a creature grappled, it can treat that creature as being unarmored for the purpose of attacking it.  
 • It is capable of traveling through any opening small enough to stick one’s fist.  
 • Cannot use armor, shields, or any item.   

**Foul Waters**: While within its own waters, a barakarihar has several benefits;  
 • Swim Speed is doubled.   
 • Can cast the Waterworking spell as a wyrding with a +4 bonus to its spell check and a bonus to counterspell checks equal to +1 per victim which calls its waters home.  
 • +4 bonus to all saves against magical effects.

**Infernal Regeneration**: At the end of any round the barakarihar receives a wound and has any hitpoints remaining, it may use its reaction to roll a d20 and compare it to its current hitpoints. If the result is lower, it recovers a single hitpoint. It must perform a rest before doing so again.

**Vengeance-Wrought Resistance**: The nonmagical attacks of creatures other than fiends. ephemera, and eldritch creatures have disadvantage against barakaihar so long as it commands an unavenged victim.

**Water Jet**: The Barakarihar can use an action or reaction to create a jet of water using water it is touching. This can reach out a number of yards equal to the creature's current hitpoints, with the optimal range being half as far. Treat as a toxic attack. If using water from its own home, treat as an acid attack instead.

**Seekers of Vengeance**: The Barakarihar reanimates the victims dumped in its water, turning them into Walking Corpses. They will follow its command without fault. They will always float on water and have a swim speed of 10 yards. The Barakarihar may use its action to have any number of its comandees take an aqueous form. This functions identically to the Hydromorphy spell, with the defense of the creatures being 8 + the barakarihar's current hitpoints.
