### Tentaquil
An unusual creature that is found only in seaborne caves. It appears like some bipedal toad creature with a sea blue coloration with bright yellow accents including a spiral design over uts belly. Their existence is doubted by many. Their collective noun is a hoax.

**Creature Type**: Tentaquils are insects.

**Size**: Tentaquills are between 2-2.5 feet tall and weigh between 110-125 pounds.

**Languages**: Tentaquils don't speak.

-----

**Hitpoints**: A tentaquil has a three hitpoints.

**Attributes**:  
[Body] 12 (+4)  
[Fort] 12 (+4)  
[Rflx] 12 (+4)  
[Will] 12 (+4)  
[Inte] 12 (+4)  
[Prcp] 12 (+4)  
[Empy] 12 (+4)  

**Run Speed**: It may run up to 4 yards per move.

**Climb Speed**: It may climb up to 3 yards per move, presuming it is reasonable terrain to do so.

**Swim Speed**: It may swim up to 7 yards per move  presuming the water isn't too rough.

**Unarmed Attacks**;

 • Smack: The tentaquil can use any part of its body to inflict a melee bare attack against a foe within 1.5 feet.

#### Special Features 

**Marine Breathing**: The tentaquil can breathe in water and air equally well.

**Slick**: This creature is unaffected by mucous from the mucous feature (or similar features).

**Psionic Blast**: Once per encounter as an action, the creature can loose a powerful burst of psychic energy. Anything within a cone reaching a number of feet equal to its [Inte] score + [Will] bonus (15 feet if no modifiers) is subject to a psychic attack.

**Puke Attack**: The tentaquil can project a foul smelling red substance against a foe within 10 yards as an action or reaction. The substance itself functions as mucous per the mucous feature, but the smell is rancid and metallic. Any creature affected by this must succeed a [Fort] or [Rflx] save with a DV of 16 or be dazed until the end of the next round as the smell overwhelms them. It cannot do so again for 1d4 rounds.

**Wyrding**: A tentaquil is capable of casting spells through wyrding. It knows 3 random basic spells. It gains a bonus to its spellchecks and counterspell checks equal to 1d4 + its missing hitpoints.
