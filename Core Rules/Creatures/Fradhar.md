### Fradhar
Appearing as a pseudo-bipedal reptilian, their face represents a massive heater shield. These extremely territorial fiends are born in the darkness of dungeons and other buildings long abandoned and forgotten. So long as one resides in a place, others are still capable of manifesting into existence there. As the years pass, their mantle (the shape of their face) becomes more defined. The plural is Fradhain. Their collective noun is a dominion. 

**Creature Type**: Fradhain are fiends.

**Size**: They can stand between 4'-7'. They weigh between 300 and 400 pounds. They are medium creatures.

**Languages**: Frahain do not speak.

-----

**Hitpoints**: A fradhain has at least 2 hitpoints.

**Attributes**:

[Rflx] 15 (+5)  
[Will] 15 (+5)  
[Inte] 9  (+3)

**Run Speed**: It may run up to 6 yards per move.

**Climbing Speed**: It may climb up to 3 yards per round, presuming it is reasonable terrain to do so.

**Swim Speed**: It may swim up to 4 yards per round, presuming the water isn't too rough.

**Unarmed Attacks**;

 • Claw: The fradhar may strike a foe with its claws. This is a melee cuting attack.

 • Tail strike: The fradhar may strike a foe behind it within 2 yards using its tail. This is a melee bare attack.

#### Special Features

**Fiendish Nature**: The fradhar's fiendish nature bestows it with a few particular features;  
 • Do not need to eat, sleep, or breathe. They do still need to relax to rest.  
 • Is not subject to bleeding.  
 • Is immune to diseases, poisons, and venom.  
 • Gains a bonus to all saves equal to current hitpoints.  
 • Is not affected by spells which would affect the flesh.   
 • Receives wounds, but no effect from them.

**Growing Power**: When the Fradhar has existed for 9 years or longer, its maximum hitpoints become equal to th the square root of the number of years it has presided in its abode. It can also ignore an amount of spell sickness for the purpose of spellchecks equal to its maximum hitpoints.

**Infernal Regeneration**: At the end of any round the fradhar receives a wound and has any hitpoints remaining, it may use its reaction to roll a d20 and compare it to its current hitpoints. If the result is lower, it recovers 1d4 hitpoints. It must perform a rest before doing so again.

**Deeply Disturbing**: The fradhar gets a d4 bonus to counterspell checks made against another creature casting a spell. If that creature critically fails its counterspell check, it receives a level of exhaustion.

**Wyrding**: The fradhar is capable of casting spells by wyrding. It knows the following spells (Arc Lightning, Burning Mote, Dragon's Breath, Exploding Sphere, Frostbite, Volt Switch,, Wicked Ray). It gains a +3 bonus to the spellcheck of the first spell its casts during an encounter.
