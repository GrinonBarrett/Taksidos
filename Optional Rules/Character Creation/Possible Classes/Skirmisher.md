# Skirmisher
You are a part time fighter with full dedication to the art. You specialize in quick battles and maintained momentum on the field. 

**Class Skills**: Select one of the following (Riding, Roping, or Survival).

### Basics of Battle
You gain training in two weapons of your choice.

### Battle Momentum
You dance through combat with unerring rhythm. The sounds of a skirmish are a music to your soul. You gain a point "battle momentum" for any of the following (Successfully attacking a creature, shoving a creature, slamming a creature, sprinting). You may have a number of these points up to your level. At the end of each round, your total battle momentum is reduced by 1. If you become prone, you lose a point of battle momentum. If you become restrained or immobilized in any way, you lose all battle momentum accrued.

Anytime you perform an attack, you may expend a point of battle momentum to gain a +2 bonus for the purpose of inflicting a critical hit. You may only do so once per attack.

### 2) Greater Training
You gain training in four weapons of your choice.
Choose [Body] or [Rflx], it is increased by 1.  
Choose [Prcp] or [Empy], it is increased by 1.  
Your [Will] is increased by 1.

### 2) Rapid Strike
When performing an attack of opportunity or follow up attack, you may expend a point of battle momentum. If you do, the attack gains advantage.

### 3) Evasive Maneuver
When you dodge an attack, if that attack was a critical failure, you gain a point of battle momentum.

### 4) Touch and Go
During the first opposed check of a brawl you are engaged in, you may spend a point of battle momentum. If you do, you may gain a bonus to your brawl check equal to your heroism. If you are successful in any degree, after applying any effects for a critical success, you end the brawl and move 1 yard using any mode of movement. This movement does not provoke an attack of opportunity from that creature.

### 4) Wild Rush
When you sprint, if you would receive fatigue as a result, you may choose to spend a point of battle momentum to prevent that fatigue.

### 6) Slip Away
As an additional option when you have a critical success while brawling, you may choose to disengage the brawl and then move up to a number of feet equal to your check's points of success (so long as it does not exceed your chosen mode of movement). If you do, you gain a point of battle momentum and that creature cannot perform an attack of opportunity against you as a result of this movement.

### 7) False Swipe
Once per round, when you perform a Feint, if a creature within 2 yards performs a reaction in response to it, it does not count against your reaction for the round.
Additionally, when you perform a Feint against a creature (whether or not it was successful), if it has fewer HP than your level, you know how many hitpoints it currently has.

### 8) Spring Step
When you side-step, the next attack made against you before the end of the round has disadvantage if you are aware of it. Additionally, your side-steps do not provoke attacks of opportunity.

### 9) Oppositional Force
Anytime you perform a check as a result of a beat, brawl, or parry, you may spend a point of battle momentum. If you do, roll a d4 and add it to your result. You may only do so once per check.  
When you brawl with a creature, or perform a counter attack against, you know how many hitpoints it has remaining so long as they are not greater than your level.

### Splash or Specialize
At levels 5 and 10, you may choose to splash another class into your training or choose instead to specialize. If you already specialized, you must splash.

#### Specialize
If you specialize as a skirmisher, you gain the following:  
Your maximum hitpoints are increased by 1.
You gain a skill of your choice or training in two weapons of your choice.
When you perform a reaction, you may spend 3 points of battle momentum. If you do, it does not count against your reaction for the round.

#### Splash
If you splash skirmisher as a member of another class, you gain the Battle Momentum feature and Touch and Go feature.  
You also gain your choice of either the Evasive Maneuver feature or the Wild Rush feature.
Furthermore, you gain training in a weapon of your choice.
