A whip as a weapon does not perform exactly as other eapons. The user gains a bonus to the attack equal to their [Rflx] bonus. Parries made against the attack ignore this bonus. In place of inflicting a wound, the foe is instead grappled. If the attack would qualify to damage armor, the foe is instead dazed until the end of the next pahse. If the attack is a critical hit, the foe is knocked prone.

If one acquires warrior mastery with whipps, they may call a number when they attack. If the attack roll result is the called number, the foe is vulnerable until the end of the next phase. Additionally, you may choose to make a foe vulnerable (until the end of the next phase) instead of mmaking them prone when the attack would qualify to damage armor.

If playing with these rules, the Shakasawt and Whip of Thorns and Frost become whip weapons instead of flails.

The whip can be a $100 weapon which weighs at least 2 lbs. It should have a range of 2-4 yards, with disadvantage on attacks within the first third of its range and the outer third of its range.

###### Whips are not included as a weapon as they are not effective weapons... however there is a fantasy with them as weapons. If a narrator wants to support whips as a weapon, they should feel fine using this.
